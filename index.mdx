## What is Trellis?

All-in-one AI framework & toolkit for Claude Code & Cursor.

The problem it solves: AI assistants are smart, but they don't know your codebase conventions. They don't know that your team uses `snake_case` for database columns, or that error handling follows a specific pattern. Every session starts from scratch.

Trellis fixes this by giving AI assistants persistent context about your project. You write down your conventions once, and every AI session gets them automatically.

## How it works

1. **Specs** - You document your coding conventions in `.trellis/spec/`. These get injected into AI context automatically.

2. **Tasks** - You track work in `.trellis/tasks/`. Each task can have its own context files that tell the AI what's relevant.

3. **Workspace** - Session journals in `.trellis/workspace/` let you pick up where you left off.

## Who is this for?

Trellis works best if you:

- Use Claude Code or Cursor for daily development
- Work on a codebase with specific conventions
- Want AI to follow your team's patterns, not generic ones
- Care about consistency across AI sessions

It's overkill for one-off scripts or greenfield projects where conventions don't exist yet.

## What Trellis is not

Trellis doesn't write code for you. It doesn't replace your judgment. It just makes sure the AI has the right context before it starts writing.

Think of it like onboarding docs for your AI assistant. You wouldn't expect a new hire to write good code without understanding your codebase. Same idea.
